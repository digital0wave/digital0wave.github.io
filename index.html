Jin Won Seo

While building my career as a server-side programmer in Korea, I was always curious about Embedded Systems (ES) where hardware is directly controlled by software. I was also fascinated by how analog signals are processed as digital data and how to deal with both signals in electronics. My curiosity in ES and signal processing led me to school again, this time in Canada, to study Electrical and Computer Engineering Technology (ECET) at the British Columbia Institute of Technology (BCIT). Learning about my favorite subjects—real-time ES, signal processing, feedback control, and mathematics—equipped me with the theoretical underpinnings for diverse applications, including interpreting data from sensors, processing signals, computing data, and controlling systems running in real time. Designing and implementing techniques—firmware, troubleshooting, and RTOS in my workplace—provided me with the practical experience required for ES development.
